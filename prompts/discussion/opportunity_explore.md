## White Hat — Facts & Data
What do we know for certain? What data exists?
- What similar tools or solutions already exist? How do people solve this today?
- What can be stated as fact about the target user's situation (from the problem definition)?
- What is unknown but could be found out easily?
Do NOT evaluate or suggest. Only state facts and data.

$slot_number == 2:
## Green Hat — Creative & Generative
Generate ideas without constraints. No filters.
- List 5–8 possible approaches, no matter how unconventional
- For each: one sentence describing the core mechanic
- Include at least one "crazy" idea that ignores technical constraints
- Include at least one "boring but solid" idea
Do NOT evaluate. Just generate.

$slot_number == 3:
## Yellow Hat — Optimistic Value
What is the best-case outcome if this succeeds?
- What is the highest-value outcome for the user?
- Which of the approaches from Green Hat (or any approach) has the strongest upside?
- What would make this genuinely delightful rather than just functional?
Focus on value and benefit. Stay realistic about the stated scope.

$slot_number == 4:
## Black Hat — Critical Risk
What could go wrong? Where are the failure modes?
- What are the top 3 risks if we build this?
- Which user assumptions from Phase 0 are most likely to be wrong?
- What technical or operational risks are being underestimated?
- What would cause users to abandon this after trying it?
Be specific. Vague risks ("it might not work") are not useful.

$slot_number == 5:
## Red Hat — User Emotion & Intuition
Respond as the target user's gut reaction, not as an analyst.
- Which direction feels most natural for this type of user?
- What would feel annoying, confusing, or off-putting even if technically correct?
- What would create an immediate "oh, this gets me" moment?
- What emotional need is most important to satisfy first?
No analysis. Trust intuition and user empathy.

$slot_number == 6:
## Blue Hat — Process & Framing
Step back and look at the thinking itself.
- Is the problem statement from Phase 0 correctly framed? Would you reword it?
- Are we solving the right problem, or are we being pulled toward a more interesting but less important one?
- What is the most important question the team needs to answer before choosing a direction?
- What should Phase 2 (requirements) focus on to avoid wasted effort?
